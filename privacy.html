2. Summary of our privacy approach

Master 1000 is designed around data minimization:

  • We do not display third-party advertising.
  • We do not sell personal information.
  • We do not share personal information for cross-context behavioral advertising.
  • We do not track your activity across unrelated apps or websites.
  • We do not collect precise location, contacts, photo library contents, or camera images.
  • Speak mode is designed to process speech recognition on the device. We do not store or upload raw microphone audio.
  • Guest learning records are stored locally on the device rather than in our cloud account database.
  • Registered-user learning records are stored in the cloud so progress can be synchronized and restored.
  • Push notifications are optional and may be disabled at any time.

“Anonymous,” “aggregated,” or “de-identified” information is not treated as personal information where applicable law excludes it and where we do not reasonably attempt to re-identify it.

3. Information we collect

3.1 Account and profile information

When you create a registered account, we may collect:

  • email address;

  • authentication method;

3.2 Sign in with Apple and Google Sign-In

When you choose Sign in with Apple or Google Sign-In, the provider may send us information permitted by you and needed to create or authenticate your account, such as:

3.3 Guest mode

You may use available guest functionality without creating a cloud account. In guest mode:

  • learning progress, responses, mastery scores, review schedules, XP, goals, and similar learning records are intended to remain locally on the device;
  • guest learning records are not synchronized to our user-account database;
  • uninstalling the App, clearing App data, changing devices, or losing the device may permanently erase guest records; and
  • if you later create or connect an account, the App may offer to migrate selected local records to the cloud, subject to a clear user action.

Guest mode does not necessarily prevent limited technical processing by Apple, the App Store, RevenueCat, push-notification infrastructure, or privacy-preserving crash/analytics tools when those features are used. Purchases, restore-purchase actions, crash reports, and push tokens may be processed as described below.

3.4 Learning and performance information

For registered users, we may collect and store:

  • exercise answers and whether they were correct;
  • word-ordering exercise results;
  • listening exercise results;
  • recognized speech text and pronunciation or accuracy score;
  • mastery score for each sentence, generally from 0 to 100;
  • Smart Recall review dates, intervals, and scheduling information;
  • XP, streaks, daily goals, completed lessons, levels, and achievements;
  • response time, number of attempts, hints, and error patterns;
  • mastery-exam responses and results;
  • certificates earned after learning milestones;
  • personal completion estimates and learning forecasts;
  • selected course, source language, target language, and progress status; and
  • timestamps and synchronization events needed to preserve progress.

We use this information to provide lessons, personalize review timing, calculate progress, generate statistics and certificates, and estimate course completion.

Certificates are internal learning milestones only. They are not government-issued, academic, professional, immigration, employment, or regulated-language certificates.

3.5 Speak mode, microphone, and speech recognition

Speak mode requires microphone permission. When you activate it:

  1. the microphone captures your voice temporarily;
  2. the App is designed to request on-device speech recognition through Apple’s Speech framework;
  3. the temporary audio is used to generate recognized text;
  4. the App compares the recognized text with the exercise and calculates a result or score; and
  5. only the resulting text, score, and related learning metadata may be saved.

We do not intentionally upload raw speech audio to our servers, add it to your cloud account, or retain it as an audio recording.

On-device speech recognition depends on the device, operating-system version, language, downloaded language assets, and Apple’s technical support. The App should disable or limit Speak mode where on-device recognition is unavailable rather than silently sending audio to a cloud recognition provider. This statement must match the released implementation.

We do not use voice recordings to identify you, create a voiceprint, perform biometric identification, train a voice model, or advertise to you.

Apple may process operating-system permissions and on-device framework activity under Apple’s own terms. Text-to-Speech playback uses system or licensed voice technologies and is separate from recording your voice.
